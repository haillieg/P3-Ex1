Dahlia (Dahlia)

Boasting intricate, symmetrical petals, dahlias are masterpieces of the late summer garden, blooming in a vast array of shapes from tiny pom-poms to giant, 10-inch "dinner plate" blooms. Native to Mexico, these flowers are popular for their vivid colors and ability to bloom continuously from mid-summer until the first frost.

Rose (Rosa)

Often called the "queen of flowers", roses are iconic symbols of love and beauty known for their classic look and incredible fragrance. They come in thousands of varieties including, climbing, trailing, and shrub forms with colors ranging from white to yellow, pink, and dark crimson.

Peony (Paeonia)

Known for their lush, fluffy, and intensely fragrant blooms, peonies are a favorite in spring and early summer gardens. These long-lived perennials come in a wide range of colors, from delicate white to lushous pink and are often used in wedding bouquets to symbolize honor, fortune, and a happy marriage.
